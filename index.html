Across the Sea: Energizing yet Tranquil Korea

What I love more than the sight of mountains and sea, is hiking :). Busan is known for all of the above, so I decided why not check out Seokbulsa Temple. I took a local bus which dropped me off at the foothills of a mountain, about four kilometers away from the temple. I thought, pssst, cinch, I've hiked way more. But damn those winding, off-trail roads. There were moments where I felt, am I even going in the right direction?

Read More

In my panic I stopped over at a mini plaza in the middle of nowhere, and I stepped in a home reno store that specializes in cupboards. They were nice and friendly... and perhaps surprised to see a random foreigner, sweating and out of breath to come out of nowhere. One of the ladies searched on her computer and confirmed, yes indeed I was heading in the right direction, and she pointed to where I should keep walking. Thank God for translaton apps.

The more I walked and got over the inner anxieties, the more I felt at peace. Even the cackling cicadas seemed more pleasant, as if telling me, you're almost there! And alas there I was. After taking a few moments to breathe and close my eyes, I felt an inner calm I haven't felt in a long time. My hunger ceased. I thought of my family, and how not so long ago I felt so ill that I couldn't get out of bed. I clasped my hands together and prayed for being lucky to be here, to see the wonderful view and ancient temple. I prayed for my family and friends to be well and happy. I guess this was my own mindful, zen moment.

More, More, More!

I love fish and seafood, I'd have it every day if I could. A friend asked me if I could give up meat. I said sure, no prob... so long as I have my fish, shrimp, squid and all the joys of the sea :D. This was from my first meal in Hanoi when I arrived in the evening. Yes, yes and yes!

Fried rice is a go-to for me, it's always delicious, whether for lunch or dinner. But to have it in a carved pineapple? NO WAY! It looked so beautiful I didn't want to destroy it by eating it. But this sentiment didn't last long... done, devoured and digested ;)
